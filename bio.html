A Little Something About Me

Since learning the ability to walk Doug has striking pots and pans and numerous other objects until he began playing drum set at the age of 12. Having first influences being in the realm of rock and roll and hip hop. Having sang since childhood in choir and around the house at the age of 16 he began singing and writing his own songs but at the same time became immersed in percussion. his first percussion instrument being a djembe.

After gaining a scholarship and attending a performing arts high school in southern california he decided to put all his energy into percussion and attended renowned Berklee college of music. There he studied with Eugie Castrillo, Jamey Haddad, Ricardo Monzon, Ernesto Diaz, Jenwood Denard and many other greats in the music industry. During his 2nd year at berklee he travled to Guinea West Africa with berklee professor Mohammed Kalifa Kamara to study Djembe and west african music.

After graduating Berklee Doug traveled to Kingston jamaica and Havana Cuba to study percussion and record samples before moving to New York City in 2011. Since then he has also traveled Haiti to work with a non profit music organization and to Rio and Bahia in Brazil where he studied with many of brazils top players and recorded music/sounds for sampling.

Doug has recorded/performed with a variety of artists in NYC and LA and toured nationally extensively and Europe, Canada, Australia, Hawaii, Mexico, and more. Including world famous venues like Red Rocks amphitheater, Both Greek venues in LA and SF, and many more.

He has released recorded samples of his playing and arsenal of instruments he has accrued over the years entitled "RARE percussion vol 1" and is releasing many more in the near future.

Doug has been carving out a name and reputation as the go to percussionist for many pop, R&B, hip hop, singer/songwriter, gospel, soul, electronic artists having many world influences and techniques mixed with the right touch and texture for todays modern production and contemporary style both on and off the stage.
